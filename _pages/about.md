I'm a doctoral researcher at the [Hybrid Quantum Systems Laboratory](https://www.hqlab.se) at Chalmers University of Technology, supervised by Prof. [Nils J. Engelsen](https://www.chalmers.se/en/persons/nilseng/). Our group is the first strontium-atom experiment in Sweden.

My PhD project builds a hybrid platform that traps a single strontium-88 atom in an optical tweezer and couples it to a Si₃N₄ photonic-crystal defect cavity on the 689 nm intercombination line. The aim is to mediate coherent atom–phonon interactions with a suspended nanomechanical resonator and use that interface to generate macroscopic quantum states. I work across the full experimental stack: ultra-high-vacuum chamber design, laser cooling and trapping, custom electronics (FPGA / Red Pitaya), nanofabrication of photonic devices, and the simulation and control software that ties it all together.

The project is funded by the ERC Consolidator Grant [SEQUENCE](https://cordis.europa.eu/project/id/101117144).

Before Chalmers, I did my master's at IIT Delhi in theoretical high-energy physics — discrete field theories on branching lattices with Prof. Sarthak Parikh — and an undergraduate degree in physics at the University of Delhi. I'm interested in industry roles in experimental cavity QED, quantum optomechanics, and precision instrumentation after the PhD.
