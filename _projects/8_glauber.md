Summer internship at the Saha Institute of Nuclear Physics (Kolkata), with Prof. Debasish Das.

The Glauber model treats a heavy-ion collision at relativistic energies as a sum of independent nucleon-nucleon scatterings, sampled from realistic nuclear density profiles. I implemented a Monte Carlo Glauber simulation and used it to compute number-of-participants and number-of-collisions distributions as a function of centrality, comparing against published LHC data for Pb-Pb and Au-Au systems.

A useful introduction to large-scale numerical work in high-energy physics.

**Stack:** C++, ROOT, Monte Carlo methods.
