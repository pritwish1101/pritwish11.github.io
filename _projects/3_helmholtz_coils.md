The strontium experiment needs MOT and bias coils delivering ~10 mT at 80–100 A continuously, with a clear inner bore for optical access and atomic transport. Off-the-shelf coils don't fit our geometry, so I designed them in-house: 84 turns (6 axial × 14 radial), 60 mm inner radius, hollow-conductor copper with parallel-hydraulic and series-electrical topology so each turn sees the full current but the cooling flow rate stays in the right range.

I validated the magnetic-field profile and thermal performance in COMSOL Multiphysics, then ran a sourcing process with two specialised vendors (TechniCoil and Custom Coils Inc.) to translate the design into a manufacturable part. The current sticking point is the cooling-circuit topology on one vendor's default tooling — instructive for understanding what flexibility vendors actually offer.

**Stack:** COMSOL Multiphysics, vendor engineering correspondence.
