A small theory project on whispering-gallery modes — the high-Q electromagnetic resonances that live at the edge of a circular dielectric. I solved the relevant Helmholtz problem numerically for cylindrical geometries and looked at how mode structure depends on refractive-index contrast and resonator radius.

A nice on-ramp to thinking about optical cavities, which has turned out to be useful for understanding the photonic-crystal defect cavities I work on now.

**Stack:** MATLAB.
